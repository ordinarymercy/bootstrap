Comparing Colors Between Two Websites

There are several key components that contribute to effective web design, such as navigation, whitespace, SEO, content, typography, and more. In this article, we will explore the use of color in two websites, and compare them. The first website is for Mailchimp. a popular email marketing tool, and the other is for Stardust Salon in New Orleans. Please allow me to take you on a journey in terms of how color can make quite an impact on the audience’s mood.





We will begin with Mailchimp. The screenshot shown above is what you'll see on its landing page. You can click the image to go to the website. Notice the use of bright, cheery yellow on its “Sign Up” buttons. This draws the audience’s eye to them, in an effort to drive a conversion. A conversion is just the goal of a website. It could be a sale, a newsletter sign-up, or a trial period for software. In this case it is the latter. Yellow and gray go nicely together, and I love the use of grays and neutrals in the blocks below.

The screenshot above depicts the home page of Stardust Salon in New Orleans. Click the image to go to the website. I find the use of dark colors to be depressing and gloomy. I suspect they were going for sophisticated, but, in my opinion, it desperately needs a pop of color. Just adding some colored text to the navigation bar would do wonders for this website. As you explore the site, thankfully there is more white on some of the pages, but the overall effect is still gray and black.


Mailchimp's home page on a phone cleverly uses yellow to emphasize the desired conversion, which is to sign up for a free trial of its software.

The Stardust landing page on a phone took a long time to load. I'm not sure that the logos of their business partners is placed in the most advantageous positions. It might be better to move the "Call Now" button up top so that the user does not have to scroll to see it. However, the use of color is improved on the mobile site, with more white space than the full page.


The use of yellow is an excellent way to drive conversions. The light orange is a nice touch, as well. It serves to delineate the footer from the rest of the page, and it goes nicely with yellow.

Once again, Stardust does not use color as effectively as it could. But, at least it is consistent.
