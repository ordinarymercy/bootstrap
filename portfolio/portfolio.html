Portfolio

Here are a few examples of my work in Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Animate.

Magazine Cover: The purpose of the project was to use Adobe Photoshop to design a fictional magazine cover featuring a made-up celebrity. I applied a mask to eliminate the background and replaced with a gradient, and added headlines using embossing and drop shadows. Also I used the patch tool to cover up my arm with a purple sleeve.

Bagel Bliss Logo: The purpose of this project is to create a logo for a fictional company, in Adobe Photoshop. Although not explicitly instructed to do so, I was careful to create a vector image that could maintain crisp lines and clarity at any size, whether it's on a business card or a billboard.

Photo Restoration: The goal of this project was to restore an old, creased black and white photograph with Adobe Photoshop. I subscribed to Adobe Stock and discovered two images to use: one for the background and the other as the damaged photograph itself. I really enjoyed this project!

Lucky Lizard Web Banner: In Adobe Animate, I utilized an existing logo of mine and added the animation of the tongue sticking out, catching the fly, and then retracting. The Animate file also has the banner turning magenta upon hover, but the .gif does not support that.

Rigging: With Adobe Animate, I used the bone tool to animate a simple figure. The purpose of this project is to demonstrate a basic character performing an animation, and I chose to have a character doing jumping jacks. This animation serves as a straightforward introduction to character animation for beginners.
